Send enough to draft safely

Best input: column headings, question text, blank answer cells, and only the generic control descriptions your team is comfortable sharing externally.

No secrets. No customer data. No invented claims.

Remove completely

  • Customer names, contract terms, procurement portal URLs, and named contacts.
  • API keys, credentials, tokens, private links, IP ranges, exact network diagrams, and secrets.
  • Employee/customer personal data and screenshots containing live internal records.
  • Security details your team has not approved for external sharing.

Okay to include

  • Question IDs, section names, question text, and expected answer format.
  • Blank answer cells or current draft answers you already send to prospects.
  • Generic policy/control descriptions that are true and externally shareable.
  • Links or labels for approved public docs, not private raw docs.

Flag instead of guessing

  • Use needs owner when an engineer/founder must verify the answer.
  • Use do not claim for SOC2/ISO/pen-test/control claims you cannot prove.
  • Leave technical specifics blank when unsure; the answer map is a review tool, not compliance advice.
